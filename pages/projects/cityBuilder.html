Welcome to Population Simulator! The point of the "game" is to try to get as much money as possible, or to just experiment. Here are the basic rules:

  1. The population increases or decreases based off of the happiness meter. If the happiness meter is below 0, the population will start to decrease, and the lower it gets the faster it decreases. That goes the same for positive numbers, with the higher the meter the faster the population will grow. Happiness is determined by if the population has enough resources to support all the people living. There are two types of resources needed: food and oxygen. You can see how to get those resources below.

  2. There is a 100 by 100 grid of land, and you can choose to place different types of infrastructure on each one. There are four types, each with their own use:

    • Cities start with a population of 20,000 and will start to overpopulate at 1 million. Their main function is to grow your population as fast as possible and as high as possible, but they cost the most.
    • Suburbs start with a population of 500 and will start to overpopulate at 20,000. Their main function is to be a cheaper alternative to cities, but they overpopulate faster.
    • Farms are the main way to get resources for your population. They grow 10 food resources every day (2 seconds) per person living in them. They cap out at 1000 people but start with 100.
    • Forests are the only way to get oxygen resources for your population. Each forest holds 10,000 trees, but they also give 1,000 extra food resources.
    • You only start with $1,000,000 so choose your starter cells wisely; if you place a city first you won't have enough money to support the population with resources!
  3. Each person needs two types of resources every day: Food and Oxygen (trees). You need 2 resources and 1 tree for every person in your population. If there are not enough resources for each person, the happiness meter will start to decrease, which could start decreasing the population.
  4. When cells overpopulate, they start to use extra resources. On top of the resources needed per person, Additional resources will be taken for extra infrastructure needed to grow the population.
  5. Each cell you place cost money, and you get money from taxation on the population. You can decide how much of their average income you want to take, but if you raise it too high the happiness meter will start to decrease.
  6. You can remove a cell by clicking on it again. You will not get your money back!

Thanks for playing! Beware, this is a beginner level project, and it may have multiple bugs/inconsistencies.
